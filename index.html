Eric Leonardis, MS, PhD ABD

Doctoral Researcher – Andrea Chiba Lab – 2015 – Present

Doctoral researcher studying at University of California, San Diego's Chiba Lab. I research animal and machine learning from using methods from systems neuroscience and dynamical systems theory. I work with Professor Andrea Chiba and Dr. Laleh Quinn on a goal-oriented reinforcement learning paradigm where rodents learn how to control robotic agents. I have managed multiple groups of students, research scientists, post-docs, and electrical/mechanical/control engineers to acquire behavioral/neural data then analyze that data using statistics, probabilistic modeling, dynamical systems analysis and machine learning techniques. Please check out my resume and CV for more details.

With a background in the history of science and Chinese language and cultural studies, I am very active in educating popular audiences about artificial intelligence and neuroscience and their histories. I am a recurring guest at KPBS Cinema Junkie Podcast and events at the SD Natural History Museum.
