Eric Leonardis, PhD

Postdoctoral fellow in systems neuroscience at the Salk Institute for Biological Studies. I build computational models of biological motor control, speak about AI and film, and serve as a scientific advisor for the National Academy of Sciences Science & Entertainment Exchange.

Research

NeurIPS 2025

Massively Parallel Imitation Learning of Mouse Forelimb Musculoskeletal Reaching Dynamics

Leonardis, E. J., Bhagat, S., Lobato-Rios, D., Pereira, T. D., Azim, E.

We utilize MIMIC-MJX, which uses JAX and MuJoCo-MJX to achieve speeds of more than 1 million steps per second through the physics and RL environment, enabling massively parallel imitation learning of mouse forelimb musculoskeletal dynamics.

arXiv 2025 · NeurIPS Workshop, San Diego, CA
Preprint

MIMIC-MJX: Neuromechanical Emulation of Animal Behavior

Lobato-Rios, D., Leonardis, E. J., et al.

A neuromechanical modeling framework integrating stac-mjx and track-mjx for data-driven control of biomechanical bodies in physics simulation, enabling high-speed imitation learning from motion capture data.

arXiv 2025
